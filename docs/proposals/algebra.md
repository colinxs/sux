# The sux data algebra — combinators over a `records[]` contract (FINAL)

Post-tournament synthesis: the verbs-first winning design (`map`/`filter`/`reduce`/`augment` as first-class generic fns over a `records[]` envelope, composed server-side by `pipe`), with every judge graft and every one of the 43 adversarial issues (5 blockers, 23 majors) folded in as design. This layer sits UNDER five sibling features and owns none of their domains: `search` owns the filter DSL (`_filter.ts`), `shop` owns the retail term→product core (`shop.md`), `teach`/`ask` own the `_kb` substrate and distillation (`teach-ask.md`), `style`/`edit` and `travel` are consumers. This doc references those as owners; it never duplicates them.

**The one structural thesis.** Every amplification cap in the tree today (`batch.ts:19,27`, `pipe.ts:24,31`, `batch_fetch.ts:21`) exists because *every map is effectful* — one element = one tool run = one upstream fetch. The records envelope splits the algebra in two and that split is what dispatches the cap problem: a **pure algebra** (`map`/`filter`/`reduce` over records) is single-invocation, fetches nothing, is cacheable like `json`, and is freely allowed inside `pipe`/`batch`; an **effectful algebra** (`batch`, `batch_fetch`, `crawl`, `augment`) keeps the sacred caps, now denominated in *subrequests* rather than joins and backstopped by a per-request ledger.

## Resolved decisions

| # | Decision | Resolution |
|---|---|---|
| R1 | Cap unit | All effectful caps denominated in **subrequests**, not joins/steps. `Augmenter.width` and `Fn.width` declare cold-cache worst-case upstream fetches (memo/KV/R2 ops included). `pipe`/`batch` sum declared widths — **except `augment`**, whose amplification is `Σ(unique_keys × adapter.width)`, a *runtime* record-count-dependent quantity with no honest fixed integer (default `1` would blind the caps to up to 60×; a static `60` would reject the golden `pipe([shop, filter, augment, reduce])`). `augment` therefore carries `width:"dynamic"` (a sentinel, **not summed** by pipe/batch) and self-enforces its own subrequest cap (`AUGMENT_MAX_SUBREQ` 100 top-level / `AUGMENT_MAX_SUBREQ_NESTED` 60 nested, §4.5) at runtime against the shared `env._budget` ledger. A legal pipe is `Σ(non-augment step widths) ≤ PIPE_FETCH_BUDGET(60)` **plus** at most one augment step (§4.6's ≤1-per-pipe rule) contributing its own runtime-checked ≤60 — the stated ≤120. `batch` may not target `augment` as `calls × width` (no fixed width exists); it either forbids `augment` as a target or charges each `augment` call its full nested cap (60) against the ledger. The **same treatment applies to `reduce op:"summarize"`** (also `width:"dynamic"`, §3.3/§6): its hierarchical llm-fold count is runtime-record-dependent and **wall-time-bound** (a 10MiB corpus ≈ 437 windows, deadline-limited not ledger-limited), so it too is excluded from pipe's Σ-width and batch's `calls × width` and self-enforces its own `SUMMARIZE_MAX_WINDOWS` + deadline cap. |
| R2 | Global backstop | A per-request **subrequest ledger** `env._budget` (`REQUEST_SUBREQ_BUDGET = 900`) is decremented by every `fanout` slot (charging **`adapter.width` per key for an `augment` slot**, not a flat `1`, so the augmenter's §4.4 memo read/write, its raw token/OAuth-KV reads, and its upstream HTTP fetches are all covered by that one width-charge and are themselves ledger-exempt to avoid double-counting — making augment's total ledger charge `Σ(unique_keys × adapter.width)`, the *exact* quantity the local `AUGMENT_MAX_SUBREQ` cap counts, §4.4/§4.5, so the two accountings can never diverge and augment's memo/KV subrequests are charged, not invisible), every `smartFetch`, **every `toRecords` handle/union/§5.5-cacheKey resolution**, **and every `llm()`/`env.AI.run` inference call** (the four subrequest-issuing paths — `llm()` is the fourth because `reduce op:"summarize"` is now a multi-call hierarchical fold, §3.3; augment's memo/adapter KV ops are not a fifth site but ride the `fanout` slot's width-charge); exhaustion soft-truncates with `meta.truncated:true`, never Cloudflare 1102/1015. Handle resolution charges its real KV+R2 op count — 2 per handle, 3 when the §5.5 combinator-cacheKey sha read runs — so the ledger measures exactly what Cloudflare counts, and the compression pillar's own `spill→handle→operate` golden path is charged rather than invisible. Local caps are fail-fast UX; the ledger is the hard ceiling no composition can breach. |
| R3 | Nesting depth | Carried on a **shallow-cloned** env, never the shared binding. `index.ts` dispatches `{...env, _depth:0, _budget}`; `pipe`/`batch` pass `{...env, _depth:_depth+1}`. Fixes the same-isolate cross-request race (the latent `env._egress` mutation at `index.ts:205` is fixed in the same cycle). |
| R4 | Two-algebra split | Pure `map`/`filter`/`reduce`: single-invocation, zero egress, `cacheable:true ttl 86400`, absent from every blocked/nested set, allowed in `pipe`/`batch`. Effectful `batch`/`batch_fetch`/`crawl`/`augment`: sacred caps in subrequests. **R4a — a wall-time-truncated `summarize` fold is not cached as a full result.** `reduce op:"summarize"` is the one pure combinator whose output is *not* a deterministic fold: its coverage is wall-time-nondeterministic (§3.3 — a 10MiB corpus ≈ 437 windows, whichever run first populates the cache covers only as many as fit in *that* run's remaining deadline under *that* machine's transient AI-concurrency, then sets `meta.truncated`). Caching such a partial under the 86400 TTL (+24h SWR grace ≈ 2 days) would freeze one transient 30%-coverage snapshot for every identical call — the antithesis of the "cacheable like `json`" purity claim (thesis, line 5) — and SWR refresh would re-freeze another load-dependent snapshot on expiry (non-monotonic coverage over time). So `finalize` makes the fold's cache-write **conditional on coverage**: a `summarize` envelope carrying `meta.truncated:true` (windows-seen < total) is **excluded from `deferCacheWrite`** entirely (recomputed or resumed until a run achieves full coverage — by cause, see below) — or, if any caching is retained, written under a **60-300s TTL, never 86400**, with the windows-covered count folded into the §5.5 cache key so a later fuller fold never collides with an earlier partial. Only **full-coverage** folds earn the 86400 TTL, so coverage is a property of the input, not of which run hit the cold slot (§3.3/§5.1/§5.5). **How full coverage is reached splits by truncation cause** (`meta.truncated_cause`, §3.3): a **transient-load** truncation (`"transient_load"` — the corpus is coverable within one 60s deadline but an unlucky cold/busy run stopped short under transient AI-concurrency) is re-runnable *in place* — a later idle run reaches full coverage in one dispatch. An **intrinsic-size** truncation (`"corpus_exceeds_deadline"` — more windows than any single dispatch's deadline/`SUMMARIZE_MAX_WINDOWS` budget can *ever* cover) is unreachable by re-running: full coverage comes only from the **resumable multi-dispatch fold** (§3.3 — `reduce op:"summarize"` checkpoints windows-seen and the partial fold to a `/s/<uuid>` handle and accepts `resume:<handle>`, so successive top-level calls advance coverage within their own 60s deadlines until complete). So "full coverage before caching/pinning" is reached in bounded steps for either cause, never by blocking forever on one lucky 60s run (R14/§3.3/§8). |
| R5 | Pure ≠ free of memory | Pure combinators get a **resolved-bytes budget enforced at ALL depths** — the "soft-truncate, never hard-die" promise holds on the top-level path too, where the union idiom lives. `toRecords` resolving a `/s/<uuid>` handle inlines that handle's blob **in full** (never sliced at `MAX_INLINE_BYTES`, `store.ts:13` — a mid-blob 4MiB cut would corrupt the un-clamped JSON R16/§5.1 stored whole) up to the CALL's *remaining* budget, and charges every resolved handle AND every union slice against one cumulative running total per call: `RESOLVE_BYTES_BUDGET = 4MiB` when `_depth>0` (nested), `TOP_RESOLVE_BYTES_BUDGET = 10MiB` at `_depth=0`. Resolution is bounded on **two independent axes**: this resolve-bytes ceiling (memory) AND the shared `env._budget` subrequest ledger (op count, R2) — each resolved handle and each union slice decrements `env._budget` by its real KV+R2 op count (2, or 3 including §5.5's cacheKey sha read) as well as the byte total, because many small handles fit under 10MiB while each still costs a KV+R2 pair (~500 × 10KB handles ≈ 5MiB but ≈1000-1500 real subrequests), and bytes alone cannot bound op count. **These are serialized-text ceilings, but the quantity they exist to bound is the post-`JSON.parse` in-memory object graph** — which for record-heavy JSON (arrays of many small keyed objects) runs several × the text size and is held *simultaneously* with the still-alive source string during parse. They therefore bake in a worst-case parse-expansion factor `F≈6` (measured 2-6× for keyed-object arrays; take the ceiling): 10MiB resolved text → ~60MB parsed graph + ≤20MB two-byte source string held across the parse + the re-stringified survivors, which fits the 128MB isolate with headroom, whereas the old 40MiB text expands to ~240MB and hard-OOMs the exact ceiling this rule claimed was safe. Nested 4MiB → ~24MB parsed, comfortably safe (only the depth-0 value and its unit changed). Re-derive `TOP_RESOLVE_BYTES_BUDGET` from measured `F` (or switch to a streaming JSON reader that never holds source + full graph at once and charges a post-parse object-size estimate) — never from raw text alone. When adding a handle's *whole* blob would exceed the remaining byte total **OR** the next handle/slice's KV+R2 op count would drive `env._budget.remaining` below 0, `toRecords` drops that entire handle (never a partial copy) and every further handle/union slice, and sets `meta.truncated:true` with a specific note naming the offending size and the remedy ("saved result is `<N>`MiB, over the `<cap>`MiB resolve budget — pre-`reduce`/`map` to a projected subset before saving, or filter the producer inline"; for byte-bounded unions: "union fewer handles per call, or pre-`reduce`/`save` intermediate sets"; for **ledger**-bounded resolution a count-named note: "resolved `<k>` of `<N>` handles before the per-request subrequest budget — union fewer handles per call, or pre-reduce/save intermediate sets") — never resolves past either ceiling into an OOM or a Cloudflare 1102 and never hands a truncated JSON slice to `JSON.parse` (which would null-coerce to a `failWith("bad_input", …)` on the very handle the design minted). Because the nested cap is 4MiB, a >4MiB saved set cannot be resolved inside a pipe/batch step and must be operated on at top level (`_depth=0`, 10MiB). `map.explode` is bounded against the same running budget; `batch` clamps per-item result text (no 128MB-isolate OOM). |
| R6 | `raw:true` on all four | The combinators are `raw` (`pipe.ts:12-16` precedent) so the dispatch/`pipe` boundary never double-normalizes record bytes — protects `uniq_by`/join keys/memo cacheKeys and byte-exact producers upstream (`kv_get`, `encode`, `fontcase`). Each reproduces only the hygiene it needs. |
| R7 | JSON fidelity | `okRecords()` uses a **JSON-safe stringify** escaping U+2028/U+2029. `SANE.normalizeNewlines` (`normalize.ts:63`) rewrites LS/PS→`\n` — legal-unescaped-in-JSON chars become invalid control chars, poisoning the envelope. Sample/stub emission audited the same way. |
| R8 | Envelope | `{records: Rec[], meta}`. Failures never in `records` — they live in `meta.errors[]` (FailCode taxonomy, `registry.ts:112`). One reserved per-record key: `_src` (provenance), exempt from "no reserved keys" so source-aware predicates survive a union. |
| R9 | Augmenter namespace | Every augmenter writes under `rec.aug.<name>` (`rec.aug.price`, `rec.aug.archive`). Kills the `price`/`meta` collision on retail-shaped records. Source fields never overwritten. Filter paths resolve `aug.*` via the shared `dig` (co-signed into the `_filter.ts` freeze). |
| R10 | Value conventions | Numeric/currency fields are plain numbers in store currency; `0`/absent = "no price" (`_retail.ts:34`). Shop's migration maps the 0-sentinel to an **absent** field via `normalizeMoney`, so `price < 20`'s missing→false semantics excludes phantom $0 rows for free (fixes `sort_by:"price"` too). |
| R11 | Missing-field / sort | A path missing from a record → clause is **false** for every op except `exists`. `filter` emits `meta.paths_missing` and `meta.fields`. `sort_by` is numeric-first (coerce both sides to number, fall back to string). `reduce` over 0 records short-circuits (never calls the LLM). |
| R12 | Union/merge | `toRecords`, when a top-level array's elements are themselves `{records:[...]}` envelopes or `/s/<uuid>` handles, **resolves + concatenates** them (`meta.errors` concatenated, `_src` stamped per slice). `records:[handleA,handleB]` is the documented union idiom, bounded by **both** the resolve-bytes ceiling (R5, memory) and the `env._budget` subrequest ledger (R2, op count) — a union of many small handles stays under 10MiB yet each costs a KV+R2 pair, so byte-bounding alone cannot stop a handle-heavy union from breaching Cloudflare's 1102 limit; soft-truncates with `meta.truncated:true` on whichever axis exhausts first. A bare envelope/handle mixed into a record array otherwise fails loudly. |
| R13 | Migration | A **RECORD_PATHS** table (`fnName → path \| wrapper`, shape-aware) replaces the generic list-key sniff. One deletable row per producer, "done when the table is empty." Row deletion trails migration by ≥`ttl+grace` (races the response cache) — cycle N migrates output with the row kept as `toRecords` fallback, cycle N+1 deletes it. `records:true` Fn flag is the destination; selftest gains a conformance check. |
| R14 | Distillation home | `_reduce.ts` (`op:"summarize"|"pack"`) is the **single** llm-fold home; `summarize.ts` delegates to it. `teach` ABSORBS `oracle`'s distillation (per `teach-ask.md` §0/R16) — there are not three copies. The `summarize` fold is **hierarchical map-reduce** (chunk into ~24k windows → summarize each → summarize the summaries recursively until one fits, §3.3), so its distillation consumers — teach's "bulky source corpora" (§8), travel's per-facet dossiers, the compression pillar's `batch(scrape,100 urls)→reduce op:summarize` — get **coverage up to the wall-time/ledger budget** (a hierarchical fold, not a silent first-24k-slice truncation into the small llama-3.2-3b — a VERIFIED HARD FACT: tiny context; a single-slice fold would pass only a handful of records and discard the rest), with `meta.truncated:true` (windows-seen vs total) **whenever the corpus exceeds what one 60s deadline of small-model folds can cover** — `batch(scrape,100 urls)` (routinely multiple MB) is no longer an unqualified full-coverage example. The fold's true binding constraint is **wall-time, not the ledger**: R5 routes the largest saved sets (10MiB top-level) to reduce, so 10MiB/24k ≈ 437 windows — 437 ≪ 900, so the ledger never fires first; what fires first is `FN_DEADLINE_MS=60_000` (`index.ts:41/:252`), because hundreds of ~1-2s `llm()` calls into llama-3.2-3b cannot complete in 60s. So the window-map is routed through the deadline-aware `fanout` (§2c) and each recursive fold tier re-checks remaining wall-time against the deadline, soft-truncating on the **deadline axis** (as well as the ledger) with `meta.truncated:true` naming windows-seen vs total — never letting the 60s wrapper hard-kill the fn with no meta. The fold **tags the cause** (`meta.truncated_cause`): a corpus whose resolved window count exceeds what one dispatch's deadline/`SUMMARIZE_MAX_WINDOWS` budget can *ever* cover is `"corpus_exceeds_deadline"` (intrinsic size — re-running never reaches full coverage), otherwise `"transient_load"` (coverable in one deadline, this run just hit a cold/busy slot). For the intrinsic case — exactly the "bulky source corpora" this fold is teach's single home for — coverage completes only across dispatches: `reduce op:"summarize"` checkpoints windows-seen and the partial fold to a `/s/<uuid>` handle and accepts `resume:<handle>`, so successive top-level calls each advance coverage within their own 60s deadline until the corpus is fully folded (§3.3), and teach pins only such a completed fold (§8) — never blocking on a single lucky 60s run. Each window's `llm()` call is charged to `env._budget` (the fourth ledger site, R2) AND counts against a self-enforced `SUMMARIZE_MAX_WINDOWS` cap (§3.3/§6), so `reduce op:summarize`'s llm-subrequest count is deadline+ledger+cap-bounded and is **not** modeled as a fixed width-1 step: `reduce` carries `width:"dynamic"` (§6), excluded from pipe's Σ-width and batch's `calls×width`, so `batch(N × reduce op:summarize)` does not validate as N while firing up to N×437 llm calls. |
| R15 | Save / compression | One dispatch flag `save: true|false|"auto"|{ttl_seconds}` mints the **single stub shape** `{saved:{url,uuid,sha256,bytes,expires}, count?, fields?, sample?, note}`. `handle:true` folded into `save`. Auto (default, threshold `AUTO_SPILL_CHARS = 200_000`) caches, under the plain key, the FULL text **below the `MAX_OUTPUT_CHARS=1M` clamp** and the **minted stub above it** (a force-spilled >1M records payload, §5.1/§5.3 — content-caching there would make an SWR hit re-`putBlob` a smaller, record-losing blob, so the stub-referencing-blob-A is cached instead); the spill gate is a **response-path** decision re-applied on **every** return — cache-miss `finalize` AND SWR cache-hit/refresh alike — minting the per-response stub (never cached) whenever the returned value exceeds threshold and the caller hasn't set `save:false`, so a large result returns a stub on hit and miss identically. |
| R16 | Finalize order | `normalize → summarize → putBlob(full text, pre-clamp) → clamp only the inline/cached stub → deferCacheWrite`. The blob is the un-clamped fidelity copy (R2 has no 1M concern); `clampResult` (`index.ts:65`) never touches it. `deferCacheWrite` is **coverage-conditional** for a wall-time-truncated `summarize` fold — skipped or short-TTL'd, never frozen at 86400 (R4a/§5.1). |
| R17 | Handle lifetime | Blob TTL is per-fn: `ttlSeconds = max(HANDLE_TTL_FLOOR 604800, fn.ttl + CACHE_STALE_GRACE_SECONDS)` at `putBlob` time — a cached stub can never outlive its blob. Ephemeral dispatch spills go to `spill/<sha256>` (R2 lifecycle GC); durable/`kb`-pinned handles to `cas/<sha256>` (permanent). Selftest asserts the invariant over all fns. |
| R18 | `batch_fetch` | **Kept** (its `as:"url"` per-item binary CAS to R2 has no whole-result substitute; `batch(scrape)+save` is text-shaped and one-handle). Its `MAX_URLS` cap is now a `width`-aware count against the ledger. `batch` also gains `over_from:'/s/<uuid>'`. |
| R19 | Filter DSL ownership | Hard dependency on `search`'s `_filter.ts`; the freeze is a **co-signed grammar spec** (operators, precedence, literals, quoting, error format, `aug.*` path resolution). The "fork if search slips" escape hatch is deleted. `filter` has no `url` mode (pure/effectful split stays truthful; url callers pipe `scrape→filter`). |
| R20 | Fn count (this layer) | **+2**: add `filter`/`map`/`reduce`/`augment` (+4); absorb→delete `grep`/`select` (−2); keep `batch_fetch`. Sibling deltas (`shop` −1 `product_search`; `teach` net 0, absorbs `oracle`) are counted in their own docs. |

## 1. The `records[]` envelope — `sux/src/fns/_records.ts`

### 1.1 The envelope

```ts
import type { FailCode } from "../registry";
export type Rec = Record<string, unknown>;
export type RecordsMeta = {
	source?: string;
	total?: number;
	truncated?: boolean;
	truncated_cause?: "transient_load" | "corpus_exceeds_deadline";
	fields?: string[];
	paths_missing?: Record<string, number>;
	errors?: Array<{ key?: string; error: string; code?: FailCode }>;
	augment?: { augmenters: string[]; joins: number; cache_hits: number; missing_key?: number };
	saved?: { url: string; uuid: string; sha256: string; bytes: number; expires?: number };
	filtered_from?: number;
	in?: number;
	out?: number;
};
export type RecordsEnvelope = { records: Rec[]; meta?: RecordsMeta };
```

Rules: (1) `records` is always an array of JSON objects with **parsed** values — never stringified-JSON-in-a-string (kills `batch`'s double-encode, `batch.ts:194`). (2) Failures live in `meta.errors[]`, not `records` — "drop failures" is a no-op by construction. (3) The only reserved per-record key is **`_src`** (producing fn/backend), stamped by `fanout` merges and unions and exempt from the no-reserved-keys rule, so `_src = 'web' or aug.price.value < 20` is writable after a mixed union (R8/R11). (4) Emitting fns wrap via `okRecords(records, meta)`.

### 1.2 JSON-safe emission (U+2028/U+2029) — R7

```ts
const JSON_UNSAFE = /[  ]/g;
export function safeStringify(v: unknown): string {
	return JSON.stringify(v).replace(JSON_UNSAFE, (c) => (c === " " ? "\\u2028" : "\\u2029"));
}
export function okRecords(records: Rec[], meta?: RecordsMeta): ToolResult {
	return ok(safeStringify({ records, ...(meta ? { meta } : {}) }));
}
```

`normalizeText` at the close boundary (`index.ts:216-219`, and per-step `pipe.ts:144`) folds LS/PS to a bare `\n` — a literal control char that is **invalid unescaped** inside a JSON string, so any scraped title/snippet carrying LS/PS turns the envelope into unparseable text and `toRecords→null` fails the whole pipe on data-dependent input. `okRecords` escapes them before they reach the boundary; because the four combinators are `raw` (R6) the boundary does not re-touch their output. Golden test: a record containing U+2028 round-trips producer→`filter`. Lands in cycle 0.

### 1.3 The lenient reader + RECORD_PATHS table + union — R12/R13

```ts
export async function toRecords(env: RtEnv, input: unknown, opts?: { depth?: number }): Promise<RecordsEnvelope | null>;
export const RECORD_PATHS: Record<string, { path?: string; wrapper?: string; split_ok?: boolean }> = {
	batch:         { path: "results", split_ok: true },
	crawl:         { path: "pages" },
	find_similar:  { wrapper: "array" },
	weekly_ad:     { path: "deals" },
	wayback:       { path: "captures" },
	redirects:     { path: "chain" },
};
```

Ordered coercion (operates on the un-migrated 89 **today**, before any retrofit):

1. `/s/<uuid>` handle URL or bare uuid (`storeRefUuid`, `_util.ts:359`) → resolve KV→R2 directly (no HTTP hop), **decrementing `env._budget` by the handle's real KV+R2 op count** (2, or 3 when §5.5's combinator-cacheKey sha read runs — this is a genuine subrequest path Cloudflare counts against the 1102 limit, R2), inlining the blob **in full** up to the CALL's *remaining* resolve-bytes budget — `RESOLVE_BYTES_BUDGET = 4MiB` at `depth>0`, `TOP_RESOLVE_BYTES_BUDGET = 10MiB` at `depth=0` (serialized-text ceilings sized to bound the ~`F≈6`× larger parsed graph within the 128MB isolate, R5) — then recurse on the text. `MAX_INLINE_BYTES` (`store.ts:13`) is **not** a mid-content truncation point: a handle is never sliced at 4MiB, because R16/§5.1 stores blobs whole and un-clamped in R2, so a 4MiB slice would cut valid JSON mid-payload and defeat `toRecords`. When a single handle's own blob would exceed the remaining byte budget **OR** its op count would drive `env._budget.remaining` below 0, drop the **whole** handle (never a partial/truncated copy of one handle) and set `meta.truncated:true` with the specific note (see rule 2 — the size-named note on byte exhaustion, the count-named note on ledger exhaustion). A >4MiB saved set therefore cannot be resolved inside a pipe/batch step (nested 4MiB cap) and must be operated on at top level (`depth=0`, 10MiB).
2. **Union**: a top-level array whose elements are themselves `{records:[...]}` envelopes or `/s/<uuid>` handles → resolve each in turn, **charging every slice against BOTH cumulative running budgets as rule 1** — the resolve-bytes total AND `env._budget` (each slice costs its real KV+R2 op count) — rather than concatenating unconditionally, concat their `records`, concat `meta.errors`, stamp `_src` per slice (R12). When the next slice would breach **either** ceiling — the byte ceiling (`TOP_RESOLVE_BYTES_BUDGET` at `depth=0`, so a few near-max 4MiB spills unioned in one call — whose *parsed* graphs would sum well past the isolate — soft-truncate at the text ceiling instead of OOMing the 128MB isolate) **or** the ledger (many small handles fit under 10MiB yet ≈500 of them cost ≈1000-1500 subrequests, past Cloudflare's 1000 limit) — stop resolving, drop the remaining **whole** slices (never a partial copy of one), and set `meta.truncated:true` with a note naming how many slices were skipped and the remedy (byte-bounded: `"union fewer handles per call, or pre-reduce/save intermediate sets"`; ledger-bounded, count-named: `"resolved <k> of <N> handles before the per-request subrequest budget — union fewer handles per call, or pre-reduce/save intermediate sets"`); the same note-with-size form fires when a *single* handle's own blob exceeds the budget (`"saved result is <N>MiB, over the <cap>MiB resolve budget — pre-reduce/map to a projected subset before saving, or filter the producer inline"`, rule 1) — the failure is loud and actionable, never an unparseable JSON slice or a silent `out:0`. A bare envelope/handle *mixed with* plain records is rejected `failWith("bad_input", "to combine result sets pass records:[handleA,handleB]")` — the wrong guess fails loudly, never silently into `[{value:"/s/uuid1"},…]`.
3. Already array/object (pipe whole-value passthrough) → skip parse; else `JSON.parse`; failure → `null`.
4. `{records:[...]}` → as-is.
5. Top-level array of plain objects → wrap; string/number elements coerce to `{value:x}` (fixes `select`'s bare-string array).
6. Object matching a **RECORD_PATHS** row → wrap that dotted path. **Shape-aware**: `split_ok` rows (`batch`) split `results[]` on `ok` — `ok:true`→`records` (unwrapped to `{value}`/`{text}` so one field name works downstream), `ok:false`→`meta.errors`, enforcing rule 2 for coerced legacy shapes so a downstream `reduce op:count` never overcounts failures.
7. Else `null` → the combinator returns `failWith("bad_input", "not records: pass a JSON array, a {records:[...]} envelope, or a /s/<uuid> handle")`.

Migration ordering races the response cache (`ttl+grace`, up to 2 days for ttl-86400 fns, served via SWR at `index.ts:285-290`): cycle N migrates the producer to `{records,meta}` **with its row kept as `toRecords` fallback**; cycle ≥N+1 deletes the row once legacy-shape cached entries have expired. "Done when the table is empty" stays the tracked metric.

### 1.4 `records:true` Fn flag + conformance — R13

`registry.ts` `Fn` gains `records?: boolean`. Producers that emit the envelope declare it; `selftest` gains an envelope-conformance check per `records:true` fn (parses, `records` is an array, failures only in `meta.errors`). The flag drives `gen-docs.mjs` categorization and is CI's truth source for which fns emit natively vs. rely on the `toRecords` bridge.

### 1.5 One path language — `dig()` (kills four copies)

```ts
export function dig(value: unknown, path: string): unknown;
```

## 2. Shared fan-out core — `sux/src/fns/_fanout.ts`

Extracts the three hand-rolled `Promise.allSettled` loops (`web_search.ts:214`, `product_search.ts:87`, `people_finder.ts:287`) and generalizes `pool()` (`_util.ts:40`).

```ts
export type Slot<T> = { key: string; run: () => Promise<T>; weight?: number };
export type FanoutOpts = { concurrency?: number; slotTimeoutMs?: number; totalBudgetMs?: number };
export type FanoutOutcome<T> = {
	ok: Array<{ key: string; value: T; ms: number }>;
	errors: Array<{ key: string; error: string; code?: FailCode }>;
	truncated?: boolean;
};
export async function fanout<T>(env: RtEnv, slots: Slot<T>[], opts?: FanoutOpts): Promise<FanoutOutcome<T>>;
```

Contract: (a) **error isolation** — a throw/timeout in one slot lands in `errors[]` (`withDeadline` per slot, `index.ts:52`). (b) **`FanoutOutcome` maps 1:1 onto the envelope** — `ok[].value`→`records`, `errors`→`meta.errors`. This IS `travel`'s partial-tolerant dossier envelope and `search`'s per-backend failure report; they define neither. (c) **Ledger-aware** — `fanout` decrements `env._budget` per started slot and refuses to start further slots when exhausted, returning `truncated:true` with the arithmetic (deadline honesty: it also soft-truncates near `totalBudgetMs`/`FN_DEADLINE_MS` rather than dying at 60s). A slot may declare a **per-slot subrequest weight** (`Slot.weight`, default 1): a flat `1` is honest only when a slot ≈ one subrequest (search backends), but an `augment` slot is one *key* whose `adapter.run` issues up to `adapter.width` (6 for `price`) subrequests including memo/token-KV reads. Augment's outer fanout sets `weight = adapter.width` per key and marks the adapter's internal `smartFetch`/KV/memo calls **ledger-exempt**, so a width>1 slot decrements the ledger by its full weight (never a flat 1 that undercounts a multi-subrequest key by up to 6×) while the single width-charge stays the sole source of truth (§4.4/§4.5). `fanout` and `smartFetch` are two of the **four** ledger decrement sites; `toRecords` handle/union/§5.5-cacheKey resolution (§1.3, R2) is the third, and every `llm()`/`env.AI.run` inference call (the `reduce op:"summarize"` hierarchical map-reduce, §3.3) is the fourth — so a handle-heavy `filter`/`map`/`reduce` composition is charged the KV+R2 ops it actually issues and an llm-fold-heavy one is charged its inference calls, both soft-truncating before Cloudflare 1102. (d) **No local cap of its own** — the *caller* owns and states its amplification cap; `_fanout` unifies the mechanics and enforces the global ledger, not the per-tool number. Consumers after retrofit: `batch`, `batch_fetch`, `crawl`, `web_search`, `product_search`/`shop`, `people_finder`, `augment`, `travel`, `teach`.

## 3. The pure combinators — `filter` / `map` / `reduce`

All three: single files under `sux/src/fns/`, single lowercase verbs, `cacheable:true, ttl:86400, raw:true` (R6), absent from every blocked/nested set. One **static** ttl and cost per fn (the dispatcher cannot vary ttl by dispatch method — `index.ts:206,248` reads `fn.ttl` once): `reduce` carries `cost:2` to cover its `summarize` path uniformly. The static ttl does not force *caching*: `finalize` still **skips** `deferCacheWrite` for a wall-time-truncated `summarize` fold (R4a/§3.3/§5.1), so a load-dependent partial fold is never frozen under the 86400 TTL.

### 3.1 `filter` — `fns/filter.ts` (+ shared `fns/_filter.ts`, owned by `search`)

The predicate DSL is **owned by `search`** (`_filter.ts`); `filter` and `search`'s inline `filter:` arg import the same two exports:

```ts
export type Predicate = ((rec: Rec) => boolean) & { paths: string[] };
export function compileFilter(expr: string): Predicate;
```

The `.paths` array (every dotted path the predicate touches) is what powers `filter`'s `meta.paths_missing`/`meta.fields` closed-loop self-diagnosis — a bare `(rec) => boolean` could not produce it (search.md decision 7). The freeze is a **co-signed grammar spec** agreed with `search` before this fn's cycle (R19) — operator table, precedence, literals, quoting, error-message format, and `aug.*` path resolution via the shared `dig`. Assumed grammar (defer to the frozen spec verbatim): `clause := path op literal`, ops `= != > >= < <= has(substring, ci) in [..] exists` (v1 ships `has` only; `~` regex is reserved/deferred post-v1 — Thompson-NFA — and returns a teaching error today, per search.md decision 8), combined with `and | or | not` and parentheses; compile errors → `failWith("bad_input", …)` naming the offending token. **No `url` mode** (R19) — `filter` dispatches only `records`/`text`(regex, the `grep` method)/`html`(CSS, the `select` method); url callers pipe `scrape→filter`, so the "pure, fetches nothing" invariant in the cap comments stays true.

Missing-field semantics (R11): a path absent from a record makes its clause **false** for every op except `exists`; `filter` records `meta.paths_missing` (`{rating: 87}` = 87 records lacked `rating`) and `meta.fields` (union of surviving keys) so `out:0` self-diagnoses a typo'd field. Output: `okRecords(survivors, {...meta, filtered_from, in, out, paths_missing?, fields})`. `fields:[...]` projects survivors; `count:true` returns `{count}`.

### 3.2 `map` — `fns/map.ts` (pure projection; `batch` stays the effectful map)

Settled: `batch` is NOT subsumed by `map` — folding the pure and effectful maps under one name drags caps onto the pure path for zero algebraic gain. `map` is the free transform `batch` never had.

Schema: `records`, `fields` (pick `['title','url']` or rename `{price:'aug.price.value'}`), `template` (`{{item}}`/`{{item.path}}`, batch's `fillToken` verbatim, `batch.ts:45`), `explode` (dotted path → one record per element, parent merged — this is flatMap; crawl stops being the only hand-rolled instance), `sort_by`, `order`, `take`, `uniq_by` (first occurrence wins — dedupe by `url` after a multi-backend union). Ops in order: `explode → template/fields → uniq_by → sort_by → take`. Because `explode` runs FIRST and each child carries a merged copy of the (possibly large) parent, an unbounded flatMap could multiplicatively blow past the isolate before projection trims it; so `explode` is **size-bounded** — it accumulates post-explode record bytes against the same resolve-bytes running budget (R5) and soft-truncates the exploded set with `meta.truncated:true` rather than materializing without ceiling. `sort_by`/`uniq_by`/`take` on a missing or non-numeric path use numeric-first comparison (R11), stated in the description so `'9.99' > '10.99'` never mis-sorts silently.

### 3.3 `reduce` — `fns/reduce.ts` (+ shared `fns/_reduce.ts`, the single llm-fold home)

```ts
export type ReduceOp = "concat" | "summarize" | "pack" | "count";
export async function reduceRecords(env: RtEnv, records: Rec[], op: ReduceOp,
	opts: { path?: string; instruction?: string; max_words?: number; resume?: string }): Promise<{ text: string; windows_seen: number; total_windows: number; truncated_cause?: "transient_load" | "corpus_exceeds_deadline"; checkpoint?: string }>;
export async function reduceWithTool(env: RtEnv, items: string[], tool: string, args: Rec): Promise<ToolResult>;
```

`_reduce.ts` is **the** llm-fold home (R14): `batch`'s `reduce`/`reduce_with` (`batch.ts:201-252`) and `summarize.ts` both delegate to it in the cycle it lands; `pack` borrows `pack.ts`'s tsv encoder (`pack` is not deleted). `teach`'s distillation calls `reduceRecords` rather than reimplementing llm plumbing, and `teach` absorbs `oracle` (no fourth copy). What the standalone fn buys that `batch` cannot: **a fold as a pipe step** — `scrape→filter→reduce` is inexpressible today because `reduce` is locked inside `batch` and `batch` is blocked in `pipe`.

- `op:"summarize"` is **hierarchical map-reduce**, overflow-safe and symmetric with the 0-records guard below. The single `llm()` path is a 24k slice / 512 tok call into a small llama-3.2-3b (a VERIFIED HARD FACT: tiny context), so folding whole/large record sets — the compression pillar's `batch(scrape,100 urls)→reduce`, teach's bulky corpora, a travel facet dossier — through one slice would pass only the first ~24k chars and **silently discard the rest**. Instead: chunk the record text into ~24k windows, summarize each window with one `llm()` call, then fold the window summaries (recursively) until one fits. This makes `summarize` a **multi-`llm()`-call op** whose call count is `ceil(resolved_text / 24k) + fold tiers` — a **runtime record-count-dependent amplification, the exact shape of `augment`'s `Σ(unique_keys × width)`** (§4.5), not a fixed width-1 step. Its **binding constraint is wall-time, not the ledger**: R5 routes the largest saved sets (10MiB top-level) to reduce, so 10MiB/24k ≈ 437 windows — 437 ≪ 900, so the ledger never fires first; what fires first is `FN_DEADLINE_MS=60_000` (`index.ts:41/:252`), because hundreds of sequential ~1-2s `llm()` calls cannot complete in 60s. So the fold is made **deadline-aware on three axes**: (1) the window-map runs through the deadline-aware `fanout` (§2c), which soft-truncates near `FN_DEADLINE_MS` rather than dying at 60s; (2) each recursive fold tier re-checks remaining wall-time (against a `SUMMARIZE_DEADLINE_RESERVE_MS` margin) before issuing its tier of `llm()` calls, stopping the recursion and emitting the best-so-far fold rather than letting the 60s wrapper hard-kill the fn; (3) a self-enforced `SUMMARIZE_MAX_WINDOWS` cap (top-level / nested, §6) bounds the window count regardless of clock, surfaced as a fail-fast pre-flight error (§4.5-style) when the resolved corpus would need more windows than the cap allows. Each `env.AI.run` inference IS a Cloudflare subrequest and is **charged against `env._budget`** (R2 — `llm()` is the fourth ledger decrement site, §2(c)/§6). On exhausting any of the three axes (deadline, `SUMMARIZE_MAX_WINDOWS`, or the ledger) the fold soft-truncates the current tier with `meta.truncated:true` **naming windows-seen vs total** — coverage is up to the wall-time/cap/ledger budget, **not** an unqualified full-corpus guarantee, so `batch(scrape,100 urls)→reduce` (routinely multiple MB) soft-truncates loudly when the corpus exceeds one 60s deadline of folds rather than either silently covering a fraction or hard-killing at the wrapper. **The fold tags *why* it truncated** (`meta.truncated_cause`), because two different causes need two different remedies and conflating them is what makes a naive "just re-run to full coverage" rule non-terminating on the very corpora this fold exists for. The resolved window count is known up front (`ceil(resolved_text / 24k)`): if it exceeds what one dispatch's deadline/`SUMMARIZE_MAX_WINDOWS` budget can *ever* cover — an intrinsically-oversized corpus (a multi-MB `batch(scrape,100 urls)`, 10MiB routed by R5, teach's bulky corpora) — the truncation is `"corpus_exceeds_deadline"`; if the corpus *is* coverable in one deadline and the fold stopped short only because *this* run's transient AI-concurrency ate the remaining wall-time, it is `"transient_load"`. Re-running in place fixes only the latter. **For the intrinsic case the fold is resumable across dispatches**, so full coverage is reachable in bounded steps rather than requiring one lucky 60s run: on an intrinsic-size truncation `reduce op:"summarize"` checkpoints its progress — the windows-seen count and the partial fold-so-far — to a `/s/<uuid>` handle (a `spill/` blob, §5.4) and surfaces it as `meta.checkpoint`; the fn accepts a `resume:<handle>` arg that reloads that checkpoint and advances the window-map from where it stopped, each successive top-level call folding another deadline's worth of windows under its own fresh `FN_DEADLINE_MS` until `windows_seen == total_windows` and `meta.truncated` clears. (teach, which owns `sux:kb:`, may equivalently drive this itself as a persistent map-reduce — chunk the corpus, pin per-chunk `reduce op:"summarize"` distillations under `sux:kb:`, then a final fold over the chunk summaries — §8; either path spans dispatches and terminates.) This makes `reduce op:"summarize"` a coverage-complete distillation home for corpora larger than a single 60s dispatch, not merely a best-effort one-shot. **And because coverage is decided by *this run's* transient load, a truncated fold is never frozen in cache** (R4a): a cold/busy first caller covering 130/437 windows would otherwise freeze that 30% summary under `reduce`'s 86400 TTL (+24h SWR grace ≈ 2 days) for every identical call — starving a later idle-machine caller who would have gotten full coverage — and SWR background-refresh would re-freeze another load-dependent snapshot on expiry (non-monotonic coverage over time). So `finalize` treats a `summarize` envelope with `meta.truncated:true` as **non-cacheable** (skip `deferCacheWrite`; a `"transient_load"` partial recomputes on the next call until an idle run reaches full coverage, a `"corpus_exceeds_deadline"` partial advances via `resume:<handle>` across dispatches, §5.1) or short-TTLs it (60-300s, never 86400) with the windows-covered count in the §5.5 cache key; only a full-coverage fold earns the 86400 TTL, making coverage a property of the input rather than of which run hit the cold slot (§5.1/§5.5). Because this amplification has no honest fixed integer, `reduce` carries `width:"dynamic"` (§6) — not summed by pipe's Σ-width and not multiplied by batch's `calls × width`, so `batch(N × reduce op:summarize)` cannot validate as N×1 while firing up to N×437 llm calls. The concat fallback still fires when a single window's `llm()` call fails. **0-records short-circuit** (R11): `reduce` over an empty set returns `{reduced:"", meta:{in:0, note:"0 records in — nothing reduced"}}` for every op (`count`→`{count:0}`) and **never calls `llm()`** — this is exactly the state a silent-empty `filter` produces. The two ends of the input-size axis are now handled symmetrically: no "confident summary of nothing" on the empty end, and no "confident summary of a fraction" on the overflow end.
- `with:{tool,args}` runs one tool over `{{items}}`/`{{items.path}}` (same contract as `batch.reduce_with`); refused when the reducer tool is in `{pipe, batch, batch_fetch, crawl, augment}` OR declares `width>1` — a reducer must be width-1 so a `reduce` pipe step stays single-fetch (`reduce_with tool:product_search` was the counterexample).

### 3.4 Cap statement for the pure trio — R4/R5

`filter`/`map`/`reduce(op∈{concat,pack,count})` perform **zero** egress; their only subrequests are handle resolution (KV+R2 = 2 each, or 3 with the §5.5 cacheKey read) inside `toRecords`, and those **are charged against `env._budget`** (R2) exactly like a `fanout` slot — so "no egress" does not mean "no subrequests," and the ledger sees them. `reduce op:"summarize"` additionally issues one or more `llm()`/`env.AI.run` inference subrequests (hierarchical map-reduce over the 24k window, §3.3), each charged to `env._budget` as the fourth decrement site — but its **binding constraint is wall-time**, not the ledger (a 10MiB set ≈ 437 windows ≪ 900 slots yet ≫ 60s of ~1-2s folds), so the fold routes its window-map through the deadline-aware `fanout` (§2c), re-checks remaining wall-time per fold tier, and self-caps at `SUMMARIZE_MAX_WINDOWS`, soft-truncating on the **deadline/cap axis** (not just 1102) with `meta.truncated` naming windows-seen vs total rather than letting the 60s wrapper hard-kill it or silently collapsing to its first slice. They are absent from every blocked/nested set, so `pipe(25 × filter/map/reduce)` is 25 pure computations — the exact case `pipe.ts:25-30`'s comment says the blocked list was never meant to prevent. But "pure" is not "free of memory or subrequests": `toRecords` enforces **two** running ceilings at every depth — a cumulative resolved-bytes ceiling (`RESOLVE_BYTES_BUDGET = 4MiB` when `_depth>0`, `TOP_RESOLVE_BYTES_BUDGET = 10MiB` at `_depth=0`, both text ceilings sized so the ~`F≈6`× larger parsed graph plus the live source string stay inside the 128MB isolate, R5) AND the shared subrequest ledger (op count) — charged across both handle resolution AND union concatenation, and `map.explode` charges its post-explode set against the byte budget. So all three resource-exhaustion paths soft-truncate with `meta.truncated:true` instead of crashing (OOM on the byte axis, Cloudflare 1102 on the op-count axis): (a) `batch(filter, 100 × 4MiB handles)` nested — the 4MiB nested byte ceiling and per-item clamp bound the memory, and each resolve decrements `env._budget` by its KV+R2 op count via the §1.3-rule-1/-2 mechanism (no uncounted subrequests, so the nested resolves cannot silently push a request past 1000); (b) a **top-level** `filter(records:[many large handles])` using the advertised union idiom — bounded by **both** ceilings: a handful of near-max spills stop at the 10MiB text ceiling before their combined parsed graph grows into a 128MB hard-OOM, AND ≈500 small (10KB) handles that fit easily under 10MiB but cost ≈1000-1500 KV+R2 ops stop at the ledger with a count-named skipped-slices note *before* the real subrequest total reaches Cloudflare's 1000 — bytes alone would not have caught the second case; (c) a `map(explode:'path')` whose flatMap would multiplicatively blow up — the exploded set is capped against the byte budget. `reduce.with` adds exactly one tool run; `reduce op:"summarize"` adds one or more `llm()` inference subrequests (the hierarchical fold, §3.3) — a runtime-record-count-dependent amplification, so `reduce` carries `width:"dynamic"` (§6, like `augment`), is excluded from pipe's Σ-width and batch's `calls × width`, and self-enforces `SUMMARIZE_MAX_WINDOWS` against both the ledger and the wall-time deadline (§2c fanout + per-tier clock check). **And because that fold's coverage is wall-time-nondeterministic, it is not cached as a deterministic pure fold** (R4a): a `summarize` result with `meta.truncated:true` is excluded from `deferCacheWrite`'s 86400 TTL (§3.3/§5.1) — only full-coverage folds are frozen; a `"transient_load"` partial recomputes until an idle run reaches coverage and a `"corpus_exceeds_deadline"` partial advances via `resume:<handle>` across dispatches (§3.3), so neither an unlucky cold-slot run nor an intrinsically-oversized corpus can dominate identical calls for ~2 days or dead-end short of full coverage. The one new cap constant is `SUMMARIZE_MAX_WINDOWS` (top/nested); the existing `MAX_STEPS=25`/`MAX_CALLS=100` and the ledger bound the rest.

### 4.3 Shop-core interface freeze — R (coherence major)

Mirroring the `_filter.ts` freeze (R19), `shop` (design #4, `shop.md`) owns and exports the term→best-product matcher; `_augment`'s `price` adapter imports it, never grows its own fuzzy matcher. Shop's real core is `fanoutRetail(env, {term, retailers, zip?})→FanoutResult{products: TaggedProduct[], …}` returning a flat product list, with best-match-per-retailer being a **separate** `clusterProducts(products, q)` step driven by shop's `FUZZY_CONTAIN_*` thresholds — so the frozen import is a **thin wrapper shop adds to its `_retail_fanout.ts`/`_compare.ts` seam**, composing those two so `_augment` never re-derives the matcher:

```ts
// owned + co-signed by shop (_retail_fanout.ts / _compare.ts, shop.md): the frozen signature _augment depends on
// impl: fanoutRetail(env, {term, retailers, zip}) → clusterProducts(products, term) → best TaggedProduct per retailer via FUZZY_CONTAIN_*
export async function searchByTerm(env: RtEnv, term: string,
	opts: { retailers: string[]; zip?: string }): Promise<Map<string /*retailer*/, TaggedProduct>>;
```

Return type is `TaggedProduct` (`RetailProduct & {retailer, age_s, price_scope, shipping_cost}`, `shop.md:61`), not bare `RetailProduct`. Including the match/ranking rule (`shop.md` R4 asymmetric containment ≥0.60 brand-confirmed / ≥0.75 brandless), inherited verbatim from `clusterProducts`/`FUZZY_CONTAIN_*` rather than re-implemented here. `_augment` passes a fixed narrow `retailers: ["kroger"]` — **`weekly_ad` is not a retailer** (it is absent from shop's `RETAIL_ADAPTERS`/retailers enum): the deal slot rides through shop's separate `overlayDeals`/`callFnCached('weekly_ad',{term,zip})` path (§4.2), not `searchByTerm`. So the augmenter is strictly a thin, cheap wrapper. **The freeze is bilateral**: `shop.md` carries the co-sign for `searchByTerm` exactly as `search.md` carries it for `_filter.ts` — the export lives in shop's seam, this layer only imports it.

### 4.4 Memo layer — decoupled from `_invoke` (graft judge0#5)

Each adapter memoizes per key independently of the riskier nested-cache cycle:

```ts
const memoKey = await cacheKey(`_aug/${name}`, { key, zip });
// read via env.OAUTH_KV; on miss run() then deferCacheWrite(env.OAUTH_KV, ctx, memoKey, result, adapter.ttl)
```

Repeated urls/terms across records/sessions are KV hits — `augment`'s N×M economics assume the wayback/weekly_ad/kroger TTLs do the heavy lifting — and `augment` can ship before `pipe`/`batch` adopt `_invoke.cachedRun`. The memo read/write (a raw `env.OAUTH_KV.get(memoKey)` plus a `deferCacheWrite(env.OAUTH_KV, …)`), the adapter's raw token/OAuth-KV reads (kroger's KV token read, `kroger.ts:12-58`), and its upstream HTTP fetches are **all** counted in `adapter.width` — and that same `adapter.width` is what augment's outer `_fanout` decrements from the **global** `env._budget` per unique key (`Slot.weight = adapter.width`, §2c/§4.5). Because every KV get/put here counts against Cloudflare's ~1000-subrequest limit exactly like an HTTP subrequest (§3.4), the width-charge is what makes the ledger *see* the memo/token-KV ops instead of charging them zero; the adapter's internal `smartFetch`/KV calls are made **ledger-exempt** so the single width-charge is the sole source of truth and never double-counts. Local `adapter.width` accounting and the global ledger therefore measure the identical quantity — closing the divergence where the local `AUGMENT_MAX_SUBREQ` cap (§4.5) saw the width-weighted memo ops while the global ledger saw none of them.

### 4.5 Amplification — subrequest-denominated, ledger-backstopped — R1/R2

```
AUGMENT_MAX_SUBREQ         = 100   // Σ(unique_keys × adapter.width) top-level — mirrors batch MAX_CALLS as a DoS surface
AUGMENT_MAX_SUBREQ_NESTED  = 60    // in a pipe/batch (env._depth>0); one cheap augment over ~30 records fits (30×archive.width2=60)
AUGMENT_CONCURRENCY        = 8     // via _fanout
```

Enforcement counts **`Σ(unique_keys × adapter.width)`** after unique-key dedupe, not raw N×M. This same `Σ(unique_keys × adapter.width)` is **also what augment charges the global `env._budget` ledger**: its outer `_fanout` decrements the ledger by `adapter.width` per unique key (`Slot.weight = adapter.width`, not a flat 1 per slot, §2c) while the adapter's internal memo/token-KV/HTTP fetches are ledger-exempt (§4.4), so the **local** `AUGMENT_MAX_SUBREQ` cap and the **global** ledger count the *identical* quantity and can never diverge — the memo + token-KV subrequests an augment-heavy composition issues are charged to the ledger, not invisible to it (the exact hole where a cold-cache augment-heavy join could breach Cloudflare 1102 while `env._budget` still read < 900). Pre-flight failure shows the width-adjusted arithmetic and the remedy that *works* (blocker fix — "filter first" is impossible when the user genuinely wants 30 rows enriched):

> `bad_input: augment 30 records × price(width 6) = 180 subrequests > 100 — run augment as a separate top-level call on the saved handle (cap 100), or reduce the record set, or use a lighter augmenter.`

The nested cap is **60 subrequests when the pipe contains exactly one cheap augment step** — this is what makes the SKILL worked example legal (`§10`): a `shop`→`filter` leaving 30 survivors → `augment with:["archive"]` is `30 × 2 = 60 ≤ 60`. A test asserts the SKILL example executes with a 30-record shop result.

Recomputed sacred arithmetic (into `batch.ts`/`pipe.ts` comments, honest widths):
- **Worst realistic pipe**: steps at declared widths + one nested augment. A pipe rejects at validation when `Σ step widths > PIPE_FETCH_BUDGET = 60` (kills `pipe([redirects×24])` ≈ 480 → rejected with arithmetic; `pipe([shop×8])` ≈ 56 ≤ 60 is admitted, `pipe([shop×9])` ≈ 63 → rejected). **Shop's `width:7` and pipe-admissibility describe its FAST tier only** (kroger/bestbuy/ebay/costco, <5s); `shop tier:'full'` (render) **self-refuses inside a pipe** (`run()` sees `env._depth>0` → teaching error, shop.md R2) for the identical wall-time reason `search` is blocked — 8 render-tier shop steps ≈ 360s ≫ 60s, unexpressible on the subrequest axis. The width budget admitting `pipe([shop×8])` is honest precisely because those steps are fast-tier. The unified `search` verb (which absorbs the deleted `web_search`+`product_search`, search.md §11) is **not** subject to this width arithmetic at all — it is hard-blocked from pipe via `BLOCKED_STEP_TOOLS` (§6, per search.md §6) because its `totalBudgetMs:50_000` fanout consumes ~50s of the 60s deadline, a wall-time cost the width budget cannot express (two `search` steps ≈ 100s > 60s even at width 30 ≤ 60). So a legal pipe is ≤ 60 fetches + ≤ 60 augment = **120 subrequests**.
- **`batch(pipe(augment))`**: `MAX_NESTED_CALLS(25) × 120 = 3000` local-worst — which **exceeds** the 1000 ceiling, so the local product alone is NOT a safe bound. **The `env._budget = 900` ledger is the hard backstop**: no top-level request can issue more than 900 subrequests regardless of the local products. Critically, because augment charges the ledger its full `adapter.width` per key — **memo read + memo write + token/OAuth-KV reads included** (`Slot.weight = adapter.width`, §2c/§4.4), not a flat 1 that would count only the HTTP portion — the ledger sees the memo+KV subrequests an augment-heavy `batch(pipe(augment))` actually issues. A cold-cache `batch(pipe(augment with:["archive"]))` (every memo a miss = read + run + write) issuing e.g. ~750 counted wayback fetches **plus** ~1500 memo-KV ops would have blown past 1000 while a flat-per-slot ledger read < 900 and never fired; with the width-charge the ledger counts all ~2250 and `fanout`/`smartFetch` soft-truncate with `meta.truncated:true` **before** the real Cloudflare subrequest count reaches 1000, never a 1102/1015. The comment states both numbers and names the ledger as the ceiling — the design's rule (every composition path states its arithmetic correctly) is satisfied because the *enforced* bound is the ledger charging every subrequest class (HTTP **and** KV memo/token), and the local caps are the fail-fast UX in front of it.

A selftest asserts `Σ` of the catalog widths against `AUGMENT_MAX_SUBREQ` so a widened `shop`-core cheap tier trips CI instead of silently blowing the ceiling.

### 4.6 Nesting via cloned env — R3

`pipe`/`batch` pass `{...env, _depth:_depth+1}` (never a `_nested` injected arg — that both races across concurrent requests on the shared binding and violates `additionalProperties:false`). `augment` reads `_depth>0` to select the nested cap; `pipe` statically enforces **≤1 augment step per pipe**. `cacheable:true, ttl:300` (min of adapter TTLs), `cost:3`.

Schema (v1, no `allow_expensive`/`retailers`): `records` (required), `with` (required, `minItems:1 maxItems:4`, enum `["archive","meta","price","nearby"]`), `key`, `zip` (`^\d{5}$`).

**Is augment-in-pipe needed in v1?** Yes — the single-round-trip `pipe([shop, filter, augment, reduce])` is the ergonomic payoff and the raised nested cap makes it legal. The two-call fallback (`pipe([producer, filter])` with `save:true`, then `augment(records: handle)` at the 100-cap top level) is documented for when survivors exceed the nested cap or a second augment is needed.

## 5. Dispatch-layer compression — `save`, auto-spill, handles

### 5.1 Finalize order — R16 (2 blockers)

The exact order in `index.ts`'s `finalize` (`index.ts:214-248`):

```
normalize (non-raw)  →  summarize (if flag)  →  putBlob(full text, PRE-clamp) + mint stub (if save resolves to persist)  →  clampResult(the inline/cached stub only)  →  deferCacheWrite
```

`putBlob` runs **before** `clampResult` (`index.ts:65`, `MAX_OUTPUT_CHARS=1_000_000`). The blob is the un-clamped fidelity copy — a 2MB render/readability result is stored whole in R2 (no KV 1M or token concern), so `save→filter(records: handle)` parses cleanly **for any blob within the reader's resolve budget** (10MiB serialized text at `_depth=0`, 4MiB nested — sized so the ~`F≈6`× larger parsed graph fits the 128MB isolate, R5). A blob larger than that budget is not truncated mid-JSON: `toRecords` drops the whole handle and fails loudly with `meta.truncated` and the size-named remedy note (R5/§1.3 rule 1), so the round-trip never silently corrupts JSON. Storing post-clamp would embed the mid-payload truncation marker and break both `toRecords` and any later combinator. Round-trip tests: >1MB fn output → `save` → `filter(records: handle)` JSON-parses; a >1M `records:true` payload (e.g. `batch(scrape, 100 urls)`) read a **second time through the SWR cache-hit path** returns a stub whose `count` and resolved-handle record set **equal the miss stub's** (a miss-only test stays green while the hit-path re-`putBlob` mints a smaller, record-losing blob B — §5.3 — so the second read is mandatory to catch the lost-record divergence); a ~8MiB records blob (near the 10MiB top ceiling) resolves in full at top level **inside a real or memory-instrumented isolate, asserting peak RSS stays under a hard limit** (a text-byte-only assertion would stay green in a mock store that never parses at isolate scale while the real Worker OOMs); a >10MiB records blob fails loudly with `meta.truncated` (never null-coerced "not records", never a silent empty set).

For `records:true` fns whose output would still exceed `MAX_OUTPUT_CHARS` with `save:false`, dispatch **force-spills** rather than truncating mid-JSON: `putBlob` stores the un-clamped pre-clamp bytes as blob A and mints the stub carrying `saved.sha256`/`url`→A and the full `count`, and **the plain-key cache holds that stub, not the clamped content** (§5.3) — so an SWR hit re-mints the identical stub over the identical blob A instead of re-`putBlob`-ing a smaller, record-losing blob B. Any residual clamp on the *inline* stub path (not the blob) is **record-boundary-aware** (drop whole records, keep valid JSON) and sets `meta.truncated`.

**Coverage-conditional cache-write for wall-time-truncated `summarize` folds** (R4a). `deferCacheWrite` (`index.ts:282`) writes every successful (non-`failWith`) response unconditionally — correct for a deterministic pure fold, wrong for `reduce op:"summarize"`, whose coverage is wall-time-nondeterministic (§3.3). A `summarize` envelope carrying `meta.truncated:true` (windows-seen < total) is a load-dependent partial fold: caching it under `reduce`'s 86400 TTL (+24h SWR grace ≈ 2 days) freezes one transient snapshot — a cold/busy first caller's 130/437-window fold — for every identical call, and SWR refresh re-freezes another load-dependent snapshot on expiry. So `finalize` gates the write on coverage: when the returned envelope is a `reduce op:"summarize"` fold with `meta.truncated:true`, **skip `deferCacheWrite` entirely** (a `"transient_load"` partial recomputes on the next call until an idle run reaches full coverage; a `"corpus_exceeds_deadline"` partial is advanced by the caller via `resume:<handle>` across dispatches rather than by recomputation, §3.3 — either way no partial is frozen) — or, if any caching is retained, write under a **60-300s TTL, never 86400**, and fold the windows-covered count into the §5.5 cache key so a later fuller fold is never served the earlier partial's entry. Only a **full-coverage** fold earns the 86400 TTL. This is the caching counterpart to §3.3's three-axis wall-time truncation: a wall-time-nondeterministic output must not be cached as though it were a pure fold, so coverage stays a property of the input, not of the first run's transient AI-concurrency/load.

### 5.2 The `save` flag + single stub shape — R15

`save: true | false | "auto" | {ttl_seconds}` (folds the winner's `handle:true` and the graft's `save:'auto'` into one name). Detected+stripped up front like `fresh`/`summarize` (`index.ts:167-180`). The **single stub shape** (used by `save`, `pipe` `persist`, and auto-spill so the model learns one resume format):

```json
{ "saved": { "url": "/s/…", "uuid": "…", "sha256": "…", "bytes": 2100333, "expires": "2026-07-15T…" },
  "count": 87, "fields": ["title","url","aug.price"], "sample": [ /* first 3 records */ ],
  "note": "resolve with any fn's url arg; a dead handle re-mints on fresh:true" }
```

`count`/`fields`/`sample` are present when the payload coerces via `toRecords` (records-shaped) — `fields`+`sample` is what lets the model write the follow-up `filter`/`map` without re-fetching (a raw 500-byte preview does not). For scalar payloads the stub carries just `saved`+a preview. **Schema decoration** (graft judge2#4): `toolList()` (`registry.ts:143`) decorates every fn's advertised `inputSchema` with the universal dispatch flags `fresh`, `summarize`, `save` so `additionalProperties:false` stays truthful and they stop being undocumented args.

### 5.3 Auto-spill — cache coherence & blast radius — R15 (major)

Default `"auto"` spills when the post-middleware envelope exceeds `AUTO_SPILL_CHARS = 200_000` — well above routine outputs (scrape's 20k default, typical batch envelopes) so it does not silently reshape a large fraction of the 89, but below the 1M clamp so it catches exactly the class the compression pillar targets. **Cache coherence**: auto-spill is a **response-path** decision, not a finalize-only one, so it fires on cache hits and misses identically. What the **plain** key caches is keyed on the `MAX_OUTPUT_CHARS = 1M` clamp boundary — the operative truncation is `clampResult` (`index.ts:65`), **not** a KV size limit (KV holds 25MiB values, so KV never bites first). **Below the clamp** (the 200k–1M auto-spill band, where `clampResult` does not touch the value): the cache stores the **FULL normalized text**, never a stub, so a plain caller is never poisoned by a 3-record `sample`; on an SWR hit the response-path re-mint reconstructs the **byte-identical** blob (same bytes → same sha → CAS-folds to the miss blob A, §5.4), so `count`/handle stay consistent across hit and miss. **Above the clamp** (a `records:true` payload force-spilled past 1M, §5.1 — e.g. `batch(scrape, 100 urls)` at 3MB): the cache stores the **minted stub** under the plain key — carrying `saved.sha256`/`url` pointing at the un-clamped **pre-clamp blob A** (R16) and the full `count`, exactly as explicit `save:true` caches under `::save`. Caching content here is impossible anyway: a `save:false` caller of a >1M result cannot receive full content (the clamp truncates it regardless), and content-caching would force the SWR hit to re-`putBlob` the ≤1M record-boundary-clamped text into a **different, smaller blob B** with a divergent `count` — CAS (§5.4) does not fold A and B (different bytes, different sha), so identical calls would return stubs with divergent `count` whose handles resolve to different record sets, silently dropping the records the miss returned with no `meta.truncated`. Caching the stub instead makes both miss and hit return the identical stub referencing the identical blob A with the same `count`, and re-applying the `AUTO_SPILL_CHARS` gate to an already-small (<200k) stub is a no-op, so there is no re-`putBlob` and no divergence. The plain-key rule is therefore **not** "never a stub" (unachievable for >1M records) but "**full content below the clamp; the stub-referencing-blob-A above it**." On **every** return — cache-miss `finalize` AND the SWR cache-hit/refresh return (`index.ts:285-290`) — the dispatcher re-applies the `AUTO_SPILL_CHARS` gate to the value it is about to hand back, minting the per-response stub when that value exceeds the threshold and the caller has not opted out (`save:false`). So "unchanged/full" refers to the cached blob **content**, not the response **shape**: a >200k result returns a stub *every* time — the first (miss) caller and every subsequent (hit) caller get the same small stub, not a >200k payload dumped into context — so identical calls return identical shapes and the compression pillar holds on the hot cache-hit path, not only on the miss path. Re-minting on read is cheap because `putBlob` is CAS-deduped (§5.4, byte-identical content → one object) and combinator cache-by-content (§5.5) folds the different-uuid same-content handles, so at most one dedup'd blob write occurs per return. Explicit `save:true` instead namespaces `${name}::save` (like `::summarize`) and caches the stub there. `summarize:true`+`save` together (R kv-minor): persist the FULL pre-summarize text as the blob, let `summarize` produce the stub's `sample`/preview, key `${name}::summarize::save` in that fixed order. **R2-unbound fallback** (R kv-minor): if `putBlob` throws or `env.R2` is absent, return the clamped full text as today (best-effort, never error) and surface `meta.spill_failed`.

### 5.4 Handle lifetime, pinned class, GC — R17 (blocker + majors)

Blob TTL is a **formula, not a constant**, computed at `putBlob` from the fn's registry entry:

```
ttlSeconds = max(HANDLE_TTL_FLOOR /*604800*/, fn.ttl + CACHE_STALE_GRACE_SECONDS)
```

so a cached `::save` stub — or an auto-spill handle minted **on read** from a still-cached full result (§5.3) — can never outlive its blob inside the SWR grace window (today's max `fn.ttl=86400` → 172800 < 7d, floor wins; a future 30-day-ttl fn → formula wins). A selftest asserts `(fn.ttl + grace) ≤ handle_ttl` over all `cacheable` fns.

**Three blob classes / GC**:
- Ephemeral dispatch spills → `putBlob(..., {prefix:"spill/"})` under `spill/<sha256>`, with an **R2 lifecycle expiration ≥ HANDLE_TTL_FLOOR**. Every >200k response minting a fresh uuid is bounded by CAS dedupe (byte-identical content → one `cas`-style object) and reaped by the lifecycle rule; SWR-refresh churn (live content → new sha → new object) self-expires.
- Durable `store` puts → `cas/<sha256>` (permanent, as today, `store.ts:117`).
- **Pinned** `kb`/`style`-referenced handles → `cas/<sha256>` minted with **no ttl** (permanent) by `teach`/`style` deliberately (R kv-major, coherence-major). A dispatch-level 7d `save` stub is **never** recorded into `sux:kb:*` — `teach` mints its own pinned handle so `ask`/`style` don't 404 their evidence after a week. The `kb`-entry schema (owned by `teach-ask.md`) records the ttl so `ask` can detect expiry.

### 5.5 Namespace inventory, combinator cache by content — R kv-minors

The prefix discipline is the full grep-derived inventory (or a single `PREFIXES` constant CI greps against), not the two-row sketch: `cache:` (+`::summarize`/`::save` suffixes), `kv:`, `store:`, `spill/`+`cas/` (R2), `sux:kb:` (teach), `sux:oracle:`/`sux:prefs:`/`sux:metrics`/`sux:*:token`/`sux:health:public`/`sux:feedback`/`sux:watch:`, `oauth*`. This layer adds **zero new KV prefixes** (`spill/` is an R2 prefix); `sux:kb:` is already covered by `kv_put`'s `sux:` refusal, so graft judge1#7 needs no code change. **Combinator cache by content** (R kv-minor): `cacheKey` for the four combinators resolves `/s/<uuid>` args to their stored `sha256` before hashing, so `filter(handleA)` and `filter(handleB)` hit the same entry when both point at the same content (auto-spill + SWR make same-content/different-uuid handles common). This sha-resolution is an extra KV read per handle arg — it is the third op that makes a handle resolution cost 3 rather than 2, and it is **charged against `env._budget`** alongside the KV+R2 resolve pair (R2/§1.3), so the ledger counts every subrequest this path issues. **Summarize coverage in the cache key** (R4a): a full-coverage `reduce op:"summarize"` fold caches under the ordinary content-resolved key (`sha(corpus)+instruction+op`); a wall-time-*truncated* fold is either not cached (§5.1) or, if short-TTL-cached, carries its **windows-covered count** in the key so an earlier partial (130/437) and a later fuller (437/437) fold never collide on the same entry — the fuller run recomputes or mints its own rather than being served the partial's frozen snapshot.

### 5.6 `pipe` persist tee + inline-args ceiling — R15/R (records-minor)

`pipe` step schema gains `persist: true | {ttl_seconds}` (a **tee**, not a redirect — `prev` still threads the full text; the step result becomes the single stub shape). This is the only change `pipe` needs for compression beyond `save`. **Inline-args ceiling** (records-minor): dispatch rejects args over `MAX_ARG_BYTES=256_000` (`index.ts:186`) while producers may return up to 1M — so `filter(records: <paste 400KB>)` fails "arguments too large." The four combinator descriptions state "for results over ~250KB pass the `/s/<uuid>` handle, not inline records"; `checkArgs`'s rejection message for `records:true`/combinator fns suggests the handle path. Inline-records is the exception; handles/`{{prev}}` are the norm.

## 6. Env context — depth + ledger — `registry.ts` / `index.ts`

`Fn` gains `width?: number | "dynamic"` (default 1; declared: `redirects` 20, `shop` 7, `search` 30, `people_finder` 6, `crawl` per its own level cap, `render` 1, **`augment` `"dynamic"`**, **`reduce` `"dynamic"`** — its `op:"summarize"` fold is a runtime-record-count-dependent, wall-time-bound multi-`llm()` amplification, §3.3, with no honest fixed integer, exactly like `augment`) and `records?: boolean`. The registry drops `web_search`(6) and `product_search`(7) — both DELETED by search.md §11, folded into the unified `search` fan-out verb — and adds `search`(30); but `search` is declared **not width-budget-admissible**: it is in `pipe`'s **`BLOCKED_STEP_TOOLS`** (co-owned with search.md §6). Even though 30 ≤ 60 makes it width-admissible on the subrequest axis, `search` internally runs a `totalBudgetMs:50_000` fanout consuming ~50s of the 60s `FN_DEADLINE_MS`, so two `search` steps in one pipe ≈ 100s > 60s — a **wall-time** hazard the subrequest-denominated width budget structurally cannot capture. `search` is therefore hard-blocked as an explicit policy, not because the accounting is absent (algebra owns the width budget; the block stands on top of it). `RtEnv` gains `_depth?: number` and `_budget?: { remaining: number }`. `index.ts` dispatches every `fn.run` with `{...env, _depth:0, _budget:{remaining: REQUEST_SUBREQ_BUDGET}}` — a per-request clone, never the shared binding — and the pre-existing `env._egress` mutation (`index.ts:205`) moves onto the same clone. The ledger is decremented at **four** sites — every `fanout` slot (a width-1 slot charges 1; an **`augment` slot charges its `adapter.width` per unique key** via `Slot.weight`, so the augmenter's §4.4 memo read/write, its raw token/OAuth-KV reads, and its upstream fetches are all covered by that one width-charge and are themselves ledger-exempt to avoid double-counting — making augment's ledger charge `Σ(unique_keys × adapter.width)`, the same quantity the local `AUGMENT_MAX_SUBREQ` cap counts, §4.4/§4.5, not a fifth site), every `smartFetch`, every `toRecords` handle/union/§5.5-cacheKey resolution (its real KV+R2 op count, R2/§1.3), and every `llm()`/`env.AI.run` inference call (the `reduce op:"summarize"` hierarchical map-reduce, §3.3) — so the "one hard ceiling no composition can breach" statement holds across all subrequest-issuing paths, not just fanout/smartFetch; a handle-heavy `filter(records:[…])` is charged the ops it issues, an llm-fold-heavy `reduce` is charged its inference calls, and an augment-heavy `batch(pipe(augment))` is charged its `Σ(unique_keys × adapter.width)` memo+KV+HTTP ops, all soft-truncating before Cloudflare 1102 rather than issuing silently past the 1000 limit. `pipe`/`batch` pass `{...env, _depth:_depth+1}` (same `_budget` reference, so the ledger is shared *within* one request but isolated *across* requests). `pipe` sums `Σ step widths ≤ PIPE_FETCH_BUDGET=60` at validation, **excluding any `width:"dynamic"` step** and **rejecting any `BLOCKED_STEP_TOOLS` step (e.g. `search`) outright regardless of its width** — `augment` and `reduce op:"summarize"` are the effectful/amplifying steps whose cost is record-count-dependent rather than a fixed per-fn integer — `Σ(unique_keys × adapter.width)` for augment (§4.5), `ceil(resolved_text / 24k) + fold tiers` llm calls for summarize (§3.3) — so **neither is summed here** (both are `width:"dynamic"`); each self-enforces its own runtime cap against the shared ledger: augment `AUGMENT_MAX_SUBREQ_NESTED=60`, and summarize `SUMMARIZE_MAX_WINDOWS` plus the wall-time deadline check (its true binding constraint — a 10MiB corpus ≈ 437 windows fits the ledger but not one 60s `FN_DEADLINE_MS`, so it soft-truncates on the deadline axis via the §2c fanout, not just the ledger). §4.6 caps a pipe at ≤1 augment step, giving the ≤120 model of §4.5 (`≤60 non-augment widths + ≤60 augment`). The sentinel is explicit precisely so no implementer defaults augment or reduce to `1` (defeating the caps — `batch(N × reduce op:summarize)` would validate as N while firing up to N×437 llm calls) or sets it to a fixed integer (rejecting the tested `pipe([shop(7), filter, augment, reduce])`, §10 — which passes because neither augment nor reduce is in the 7-plus-sum). `batch` sums `calls × target.width` (extends `NESTED_FANOUT_TOOLS`) but **cannot express a `width:"dynamic"` target as `calls × fixed-width`**: it therefore either forbids `augment`/`reduce op:"summarize"` as a batch target or charges each such call its full nested cap (augment 60; summarize `SUMMARIZE_MAX_WINDOWS`) against the ledger rather than a per-call width — so `batch(N × reduce op:summarize)` does **not** validate as N×1 (§3.3) while firing up to N×437 llm calls, mirroring §4.5's `batch(pipe(augment))`. A concurrency selftest runs two dispatches asserting depth/budget isolation.

## 8. Feature retrofit notes (one paragraph each)

**search (#1).** Producer + co-owner of the substrate: emits `{records: SearchResult[], meta:{errors}}` natively (`records:true`, no coercion path); backend fan-out is a direct `fanout()` call with backend names as slot keys; its inline `filter:` arg compiles via the **shared** `_filter.compileFilter` — `search` owns `_filter.ts` and the co-signed grammar freeze, this layer only imports it. `filter` inline is principled (R19/§9): the predicate applies **per-backend BEFORE the `limit` truncation**, so a post-hoc `filter` would see only survivors of the cut — that ordering is contract, written into the freeze, not implementation accident.

**shop (#4).** Bidirectional: `shop` consumes `_fanout` (its retailer fan) and **owns + co-signs** `searchByTerm` (§4.3) — a thin `fanoutRetail`+`clusterProducts` wrapper it adds to its `_retail_fanout.ts`/`_compare.ts` seam, returning `Map<retailer, TaggedProduct>`, which `_augment`'s `price` adapter imports at the frozen signature. Shop's output migrates to `{records: TaggedProduct[], meta}` and its producer **maps the 0-price sentinel to an absent field** via `normalizeMoney` (`_retail.ts:34`), so `filter where:"aug.price.value < 20"` and `sort_by:"aug.price.value"` exclude phantom $0 rows for free (R10). `pipe([shop, filter, augment with:["archive"], reduce op:"pack"])` is the canonical grocery-run query.

**teach/ask (#2).** Pure consumer: distillation is `reduceRecords(op:"summarize"|"pack")` — `teach` calls `_reduce`, does not reimplement llm plumbing, and **absorbs `oracle`** (per `teach-ask.md` §0/R16, which also sweeps `sux:oracle:*`). Bulky source corpora persist via **pinned** (no-ttl) handles recorded in `sux:kb:` (never a 7d dispatch stub, §5.4); `ask` can return evidence as records so the caller can `filter`/`map` them. Because distillation is a `reduce op:"summarize"` fold that can wall-time-truncate (§3.3), `teach` must treat a `meta.truncated:true` distillation as **non-final** and never pin a partial-coverage summary into durable `sux:kb:` (else a truncated fold becomes permanent knowledge). How it reaches full coverage before pinning **splits by `meta.truncated_cause`** (R4a/R14), and the split is load-bearing precisely because teach's flagship home for "bulky source corpora" is a fold that truncates *harder* the bigger the corpus — a blanket "re-run to full coverage before pinning" rule never terminates on exactly those inputs (full coverage is unreachable in any single 60s dispatch, so teach could neither pin nor complete). So: a **`"transient_load"`** truncation (corpus coverable in one deadline, an unlucky run stopped short) teach simply **re-runs in place** until a run lands full coverage, then pins. A **`"corpus_exceeds_deadline"`** truncation (an intrinsically-oversized corpus — the common case for bulky corpora) teach pins **only via a multi-dispatch coverage-complete path**, never a single-dispatch re-run: it drives the resumable fold to completion (`reduce op:"summarize"` → `meta.checkpoint` handle → `resume:<handle>` on successive calls until `meta.truncated` clears, §3.3), *or*, since teach owns `sux:kb:`, runs a persistent map-reduce itself — chunk the corpus, pin per-chunk distillations under `sux:kb:`, then a final fold over the chunk summaries. Either advances coverage in bounded steps across dispatches and pins only the completed distillation, so teach's large-corpus capability terminates on the inputs that most need it instead of looping forever on a rule that assumes truncation is always a transient re-runnable fluke.

**style/edit (#3).** Same substrate as teach: exemplar sets are records envelopes persisted under **pinned** handles referenced from `sux:kb:`; `edit`'s doc I/O is scalar text (pipe-threaded, not records). Its only obligations: emit the envelope for exemplar lists and use `_reduce` for exemplar summarization.

**travel (#5).** The facet fan-out is `fanout()` with facets as slots; its partial-tolerant dossier envelope **is** `FanoutOutcome` mapped to `records` + `meta.errors` — travel defines facet *content*, not a fan-out or envelope of its own. Dossier compression is `reduce(op:"summarize")` per facet plus `save:true` (pinned if durable); option lists ride the envelope so `filter`/`map(sort_by:"price", take:5)` work post-hoc without re-fetching.

## 9. Ergonomics vs purity — the ruling

**Principle: an inline flag is justified only when pushdown changes semantics; everything else composes via `pipe` at identical round-trip cost.** `filter` inside `search` changes semantics (pre-`limit`, R19) → `search`-shaped verbs keep an inline `filter:` (and `fields:`). `augment`, `reduce`/summarize, `pack`, persist are order-independent with truncation → `pipe([verb, augment, reduce])` is bit-identical to inline flags at one round-trip, so verbs get **no** `augment:`/`compress:` flags (no M×N flag matrix across five verbs). The universal dispatch flags (`fresh`, `summarize`, `save`) are the sanctioned exception class — they operate on *any* result uniformly at the boundary, implemented once, now schema-decorated so they are documented.

## Deliberate scope cuts

- **`filter` has no `url` mode.** Absorbing `grep`/`select` covers text/regex and html/CSS only; url callers pipe `scrape→filter` (one extra step, no new fetch semantics). Keeps the pure/effectful split literally true in the cap comments and description strings.
- **v1 augmenter catalog is cheap-only.** `final_url`, the render price-tier, and `allow_expensive`/`retailers` are cut; `deals` is merged into `price`. A per-row headed-browser render storm is the exact hole the caps exist to close, and render pricing on chosen items is `shop`'s job.
- **No `kv://` scheme.** The existing CAS `/s/<uuid>` handle is the handle — it already dedupes, streams, self-expires, and resolves KV→R2 without an HTTP hop. A second resolver would duplicate all of it and hit KV's 25MiB ceiling R2 avoids.
- **`batch` is not renamed to `map`; `json`/`pack`/`summarize` keep their surfaces.** Folding effectful and pure maps under one name drags caps onto the pure path; `json` is a target-named converter and stays; `pack`/`summarize` keep direct surfaces while `_reduce` owns the shared implementation.
- **`pipe`/`batch` do not adopt `_invoke.cachedRun` in the same cycle they gain it.** Nested response-cache participation is a behavior change flagged for its own cycle; `augment`'s §4.4 memo is the decoupled path that lets the join ship first.
- **No new global cap constant beyond the ledger.** Local caps stay per-tool for fail-fast UX; the single `env._budget` ledger is the one structural addition that makes composed, locally-legal caps fail safe instead of hitting Cloudflare 1102.
