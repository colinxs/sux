## Final coherence verdict
I've now read every proposal in full, including the build-order tails and co-signed-change appendices where the cross-document contracts actually bind. Here is the final-pass judgment.

## (1) Remaining cross-document contradictions / unresolved risks a reader must know

**A. The one hard contradiction: retail `renderTimeoutMs` is 55s in search.md but 80s in shop.md — and shop.md's own R1 proves 55s wrong.**
This is the sharpest residual defect. `shop.md` R1 and the `_retail_fanout.ts` seam signature (§1) set the render-tier `renderTimeoutMs` default to **80_000** ("≥ the cold-walmart 2-load max ~75s… **NOT** adapter-native ~55s, which is below that max"), and R1 explicitly argues that 55s cannot let a cold walmart (2 loads, 45–75s) complete-and-cache, breaking the re-call-for-a-hit contract. But `search.md` repeatedly cites the inherited retail cap as "the `shop.md`-frozen adapter-native **55_000**" (§2, §4.3, §4, §6) and builds its whole google-38s-vs-retail distinction on it — even asserting a cold retail render at 55s "survives to completion and caches under waitUntil," the exact claim shop.md R1 falsifies. Its §14 test encodes the stale value ("inherits shop's frozen 55s"). Mitigating fact: search's actual `fanoutRetail` call *omits* `renderTimeoutMs`, so at runtime it inherits shop's real default (80s) and the code is fine — but every number, invariant, and test in search.md's retail-timeout reasoning is contradicted by shop.md's final decision and must be rewritten to 80s. A reader trusting search.md's arithmetic would mis-specify.

**B. `shop tier:'full'` is a pipe wall-time hazard that the width budget can't catch, yet it is un-blocked and used as the canonical pipe head.**
`algebra.md` §6 hard-blocks `search` from `pipe` (`BLOCKED_STEP_TOOLS`) with sound reasoning: its ~50s internal fanout is a *wall-time* hazard the subrequest-denominated width budget "structurally cannot capture." But `shop tier:'full'` runs an identical ~45s internal soft-deadline, is **not** blocked, is declared `width:7`, and `pipe([shop, filter, augment, reduce])` is algebra's marquee example. On the default fast tier (<5s) this is fine, but `pipe([shop tier:'full', augment(archive), reduce op:summarize])` can stack 45s + effectful fetches + an llm-fold past the 60s deadline by exactly the mechanism used to justify blocking search. The asymmetry is unresolved — either shop needs the same wall-time carve-out on `tier:'full'`, or the reasoning for blocking search is incomplete.

**C. platform-upgrades asserts consumer relationships its siblings never co-sign.**
`platform-upgrades.md` D7 states `_entity.ts` is "consumed by `shop`'s `_compare` and `teach`'s dedup," and D4/§1.4 wire the mac-llm tier and the `schedulable` allowlist through many sibling fns. But `shop.md` §4 defines all its entity resolution (`clusterProducts`, `extractModelKeys`, containment thresholds) natively with no seam or reference to `_entity.ts`, and `teach-ask.md` defines its own `content_hash`/`canonicalize` dedup with no such reference. These are one-directional claims platform-upgrades makes on siblings that the siblings' frozen designs do not ratify — unlike the genuinely bilateral co-signs (`_filter.ts`, `searchByTerm`, `Fn.staleGrace`), which both sides explicitly acknowledge. Integration is plausible but the seam is unbuilt and un-agreed.

**D. Lesser items:** (i) `_kb.ts` ownership language differs — teach-ask §2 says "whichever feature ships first owns the file," style-edit §3 says ownership is "unconditional, not first-lander"; they converge only because teach-ask is expected to land first. (ii) Fn-count deltas are each computed independently against 89 and never reconciled into one total (my sum is ~96 against the lifted cap); notably the single gated `product_search` deletion is claimed against 89 in *both* search.md (→87) and shop.md (→88), so a naive delta-sum double-counts it. (iii) No unified cross-document build order exists — the piecewise DAG (algebra cycles 0–1 → search cycle 1; shop step 1 → search cycle 4; search cycle 0 `_filter` → algebra filter fn; shop step 4.5 `searchByTerm` → algebra cycle 7) is satisfiable and I found no cycle, but it is never assembled into one master sequence a reader can verify as a whole.

## (2) Verdict

The corpus is substantially coherent and buildable as one program: the load-bearing shared substrate — `_records.ts`/`_fanout.ts`, the `_filter.ts` grammar, the `_retail_fanout.ts` seam, `searchByTerm`, the `env._budget` ledger, `Fn.staleGrace`/`width`/`records`, and the `env._fresh`/`bustRenders` freshness contract — is bilaterally co-signed with matching signatures across the docs that touch it, and the hardest platform constraints (60s deadline, 24h stale-grace, concurrency-1 solver) are respected consistently everywhere. Ship it after correcting search.md's "55s" to 80s, deciding shop's `tier:'full'` pipe status, and having shop/teach either co-sign or explicitly decline the `_entity.ts` consumer claim.
